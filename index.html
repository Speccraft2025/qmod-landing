The Q-MOD MIDI Bridge Core (MMBC)

How Q-MOD eliminates configuration errors by solving DAW limitations directly at the system-level.

Three-Port Virtual Separation

Instead of sharing ports, MMBC registers three distinct endpoints. Port A emits pure MIDI notes only, Port B maps strictly to Mackie MCU control parameters, and Port C acts as the unified Internal Router.

Hard-Gating State Machine

A physical gate that monitors Q-MOD's mode. In Keys mode, any Mixer note is mathematically destroyed at the packet layer. In Mixer mode, musical leakage is strictly blocked. Crossover is physically impossible.

Real-Time Forensic Monitor

A passive, zero-overhead byte validator that inspects every outgoing MIDI packet before transmitting it to CoreMIDI, instantly flagging any routing violations or domain leakage.

The Manifesto

Reimagining the Laptop as a Living Instrument

In 1983, the world agreed on a protocol that would democratize music technology forever. But for the next four decades, standard laptop musicians were relegated to clicking notes with a mouse or carrying bulky external plastic keyboards just to sketch out a chord or balance a fader.

Q-MOD is built on a simple, radical philosophy: the computer you already own is a premium, high-fidelity physical instrument.

By leveraging low-level macOS session taps, custom CoreMIDI virtual endpoint injection, and advanced HSL-tailored expression algorithms, Q-MOD translates your tactile relationship with your laptop keyboard and trackpad into seamless, high-definition musical intent. No clutter. No adapters. Just pure, immediate control surface emulations.
